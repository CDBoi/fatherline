Fatherline is an interactive phone line about fatherhood. It's a living library on which participants listen to and add stories.

Created by artist/designer Jack Hardiker for Manchester International Festival Creative50, Fatherline collates personal stories about fatherhood via the medium of an interactive phone line. In offering anonymity and a common technology, the project hopes to capture an intimacy and frankness that might be denied in traditional interview forms.
Contact: fatherlinemif@gmail.com

Created by Jack Hardiker, Harry, and Craig de Gozdawa. Voiceover: Christian Payne. Music: Philip Kay. Text editing: Alex Solo & Alexandra Pereira. Thanks to: MIF, Creative50, Nexmo, Tui Interactive Media, and all the participants.

*Normal network operator rates apply. By leaving a story you give consent that Fatherline, MIF and their partners, sponsors and funders have the right to use the recording in any media, now and in the future, for the purpose of publicising Fatherline, MIF and Creative50. Please don’t disclose confidential, or offensive material, and omit names and specific locations. Not all stories will be featured.

What is FatherLine?*

Fatherline is an anonymous place to share stories, and hear stories about fatherhood. We provide a safe space for storytelling and listening, and we’d love to hear from you today. Call now for free, and follow the operator instructions. If you don’t make the call, you’ll never know.

*Fatherline is an open collaborative art project supported by Manchester International Festival Creative50. Fatherline is a space to share stories - not a support line. This website is not fully functional. Click here to find out more about the project.

Dad?

Had a dad?

Neither?

Call now. Everyone has their own unique family dynamic. It’s what makes each one so beautifully individual. Perhaps not having a father shaped an aspect of your life, perhaps you consider a close friend to be a father figure: whatever your story we want you to share it. Alternatively, call and listen to other stories.

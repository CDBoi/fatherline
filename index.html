Fatherline is an interactive telephone line about fatherhood. It's a living library on which participants can listen to and add short stories.

Created by artist/designer Jack Hardiker for MIF Creative50, Fatherline collates personal stories about fatherhood via the medium of an interactive telephone line. In offering anonymity and a common technology, the project hopes to capture an intimacy and frankness that might be denied in traditional interview forms.

Fatherline is an open collaborative response to Fatherland: ‘a bold, ambitious show about contemporary fatherhood’ by Scott Graham, Karl Hyde, and Simon Stephens, Fatherland takes inspiration from conversations with fathers and sons from the trio’s hometowns.

Created by Jack Hardiker and Harry. Front-end development: Craig de Gozdawa. Voiceover: Christian Payne. Music: Philip Kay. Text editing; Alex Solo & Alexandra Pereira. Thanks to: MIF, Creative50, Nexmo, Tui Interactive Media, and all the Fatherland participants.

*Normal network operator rates apply. Please don’t disclose confidential, or offensive material, and omit names and specific locations. Not all stories will be featured.

What is FatherLine?*

FatherLine is a anonymous place to share stories, and hear stories about dads. Whether you are one, have one, or know one - FatherLine is a safe community of storytelling and listening, and we’d love to hear from you today. Call now and follow the operator instructions. Click here to call!

*FatherLine is not a charity, nor is it a genuine support line. FatherLine is an interactive art project, in response to Fatherland, part of Manchester International Festival.

Fatherline is an interactive telephone line about fatherhood. It's a living library on which participants can listen to and add short stories.

Created by artist/designer Jack Hardiker for Manchester International Festival Creative50, Fatherline collates personal stories about fatherhood via the medium of an interactive telephone line. In offering anonymity and a common technology, the project hopes to capture an intimacy and frankness that might be denied in traditional interview forms.

Fatherline is an open collaborative response to Fatherland: ‘a bold, ambitious show about contemporary fatherhood’ by Scott Graham, Karl Hyde, and Simon Stephens, Fatherland takes inspiration from conversations with fathers and sons from the trio’s hometowns.

Created by Jack Hardiker. Front-end development: Craig de Gozdawa. Back-end development: Harry Moynehan. Voiceover: Christian Payne. Music: Philip Kay. Text editing; Alex Solo & Alexandra Pereira. Thanks to: Manchester International Festival, Creative50, Nexmo, Tui Interactive Media, and all the Fatherland participants.

Please don’t disclose confidential, or offensive material, and omit names and specific locations. Not all stories will be featured.

What is FatherLine?*

Fatherline is an anonymous place to share stories, and hear stories about fatherhood. We provide a safe space for storytelling and listening, and we’d love to hear from you today. Call now for free, and follow the operator instructions. If you don’t make the call, you’ll never know.

Neither?

Call now. Everyone has their own unique family dynamic. It’s what makes each one so beautifully individual.
